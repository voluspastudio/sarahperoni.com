01 · Overview

A mountain experience for people who cannot reach the mountain.

MNTA is an experiential brand and digital platform that brings mountain environments to people who may not be able to access them physically because of disability, chronic illness, caregiving, location, or circumstance.

The project is not a hiking app or outdoor lifestyle brand. It is designed as a slow, cinematic environment where users can move through a mountain, learn its weather and ecology, listen to its soundscape, and send a piece of that place to someone else.

Disciplines
Brand identity, UX/UI, interactive design, print, environmental design.

02 · Brand
M N T A

A mark built from facets.

The MNTA identity is based on the geometry of mountains: planes of light, shadow, weather, and horizon. The wordmark abstracts that structure into a faceted peak system, while the visual language stays quiet, spacious, and restrained.

The palette is built from dusk, stone, shadow, ice, parchment, and a single warm horizon accent. Most of the system remains cool and atmospheric so moments of warmth feel intentional.

  • Faceted mark inspired by mountain geometry
  • Cool atmospheric palette with one warm horizon accent
  • Editorial typography for quiet, reflective copy
  • Minimal interface elements so the environment remains central
03 · Color Palette

Cold tones, warm horizon.

The palette comes from one specific evening: a long ridge line, the last bit of light dropping behind it. Deep blues, mountain shadow, stone, and one warm strip at the horizon. Parchment holds the type. The system stays mostly monochromatic on purpose, so when warmth shows up, it has weight.

05 · Experience

The mountain becomes the interface.

The digital platform lets users choose a mountain and move through it slowly. Instead of optimizing for speed, goals, or performance, the interface supports observation: vantage points, weather, flora, fauna, sound, and stillness.

Users can explore a mountain from different positions, hover across the landscape to identify natural details, listen to ambient field recordings, or send a selected mountain to someone else as a digital or printed postcard.

  • Mountain library organized by range, mood, and time of day
  • Interactive viewer with vantage points, sound, weather, and ecology
  • Hover states reveal flora, fauna, stone, and environmental details
  • “Send a Mountain” feature turns the experience into a shareable message
  • UI stays minimal so the landscape remains the focus

Ecology Layer.

MNTA uses ecology callouts to make each mountain feel specific. Instead of describing the landscape in broad scenic language, the interface identifies plants, animals, weather patterns, and terrain details that help users understand what makes each environment different.

  • Flora and fauna callouts tied to each mountain
  • Short descriptions written for non-hikers and visual learners
  • Weather, terrain, and habitat details layered into the viewer
  • Designed to make the mountain feel observable, not just decorative

06 · Print

The mountains in print.

The print system gives MNTA a physical presence beyond the screen. Books, pamphlets, cards, postcards, and posters translate the platform into objects that can be held, mailed, displayed, or left behind in hospitals, libraries, galleries, and waiting spaces.

The materials are designed to feel calm and durable rather than promotional.

  • The Mountains: 64-page printed book organized by range and chapter
  • Foldout pamphlet introduces the MNTA experience in four panels
  • Handout card set explains key mountains, sounds, and access points
  • Posters use a single image and line of copy for quiet public visibility
  • Postcards support the “Send a Mountain” feature offline

The Mountains.
One range per chapter.

A perfect-bound, 64-page printed book. Each chapter holds a single mountain: a full-bleed photograph, a quiet essay, an annotated illustration of the flora and fauna, and the names of its weather. The physical object MNTA hands to a hospital, a hospice, a school library.

07 · Candle

The smell of a specific evening.

A candle, packaged like print. Each scent is sourced from a single place at a single hour — the literal needles, soils, and stone of one named ridge. The label reads like a chapter heading. The vessel sits dark on a shelf until lit.

  • One scent per named place
  • Sourced from real materials, not generic notes
  • Smoked glass vessel, ~60-hour burn
  • Six places, six hours — one series
08 · Spatial

A quiet route through the building.

The spatial system brings MNTA into hospitals, hospice environments, memory-care wings, libraries, and galleries. Floor decals, door signs, wall banners, and vinyl graphics guide visitors toward the Quiet Room without making the experience feel clinical or over-explained.

The goal is to make the path feel calm, clear, and intentional.

  • Floor decals guide visitors toward the Quiet Room
  • Wall banners create atmosphere without overwhelming the space
  • Door signs identify the experience with quiet visual language
  • Directional graphics support access in hospitals and public environments
  • System works for temporary installs, care spaces, and gallery settings
Environmental — wayfindingHospital install · system

Hospital Wayfinding System

A site-specific signage system drawn from the same typographic language as the MNTA platform. The system includes floor decals, wall banners, vinyl graphics, door signs, and directional markers.

Designed for children’s hospital atriums, memory-care wings, hospice environments, and gallery settings.

The Quiet Room.
A 10 × 10 mountain, indoors.

The Quiet Room is a temporary installation built from one projected mountain loop, one bench, two pairs of headphones, and one line of printed text. It gives people a place to sit with the mountain without requiring travel, physical effort, or a long explanation.

  • 10 × 10 foot pop-up installation
  • Projected mountain loop with ambient sound
  • Bench and headphones for seated viewing
  • Designed for hospital lobbies, memory-care wings, libraries, and galleries
  • Self-contained setup with minimal staffing needs

09 · Kit

Everything a care space needs to use MNTA.

The partnership kit is a mailable system for hospitals and care environments. A single charcoal slipcase contains the printed book, tablet stand, headphone hooks, staff instruction card, and postcard stack.

The kit makes MNTA usable without requiring a team to understand the full brand first.

  • Charcoal slipcase for storage and transport
  • Printed book and postcard stack
  • Tablet stand for at-bed viewing
  • Headphone hooks for patient rooms or shared spaces
  • Staff card explains setup and use in plain language
System — partnership kitSlipcase · mailable

Hospital Partnership Kit

A single charcoal slipcase shipped to a partnering ward.

Inside: The Mountains book, a tablet stand, headphone hooks for at-bed viewing, a printed staff card explaining how the program works, and a stack of postcards.

Purpose
To make MNTA immediately usable in pediatric oncology, hospice, memory care, and other spaces where patients may have time, but not access.
10 · Outcome
— Reflection

The mountain is yours.

MNTA reframes access to nature as an experience system. It combines brand identity, interface design, print, and environmental installation to make mountain environments available to people whose bodies, locations, or circumstances keep them away from the trail.

It is less about reaching the summit and more about making the feeling of being there available.

A year of standing very still.
