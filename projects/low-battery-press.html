02 · what it is

A journal disguised
as a desktop.

Low Battery Press turns journaling into a small personal operating system.

Instead of one blank page, users get a desktop of tools for different kinds of emotional documentation: typed entries, webcam check-ins, loose notes, drawings, mood symbols, and saved fragments.

  • Built for low-energy journaling
  • Uses familiar desktop metaphors: files, folders, windows, icons, and archives
  • Supports typing, speaking, drawing, and camera-based reflection
  • Turns entries into analog-style logs, receipts, cards, and zines

A typed journal window for traditional entries, short fragments, or generated prompts.

A webcam confessional. Tone, pauses, and repeated words become a tiny written log.

A low-pressure place for thoughts that are too small for a full entry.

A simple sketch space for symbols, scribbles, mood marks, and rough visual thoughts.

A playful expressive tool inspired by early desktop paint apps.

Mood Meter

Signal, not score.

A battery-style emotional status tool. Battery levels and signal states instead of clinical labels.

Archive

The folder.

Where every saved log, note, drawing, and generated output lives.

Print Dump

Exports your entries into receipts, sticker sheets, mood cards, or a tiny monthly zine.
